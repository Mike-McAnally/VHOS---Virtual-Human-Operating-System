# Life timeline — key dated events

- class: MODEL-KNOWLEDGE (see _PROVENANCE-HEADER.md)
- affect_significance: scaffolding — dates that let @AS_OF do its work

1.  [v] b. 23 June 1912, Maida Vale, London; father in Indian Civil
    Service; raised largely in foster households while parents abroad.
2.  [v] 1926, first day at Sherborne School coincided with the General
    Strike: rode his bicycle ~60 miles from Southampton, alone, staying
    overnight en route, to arrive on time. Reported in the local press.
    Determination-against-circumstance, age thirteen.
3.  [v] 13 February 1930: death of Christopher Morcom (bovine
    tuberculosis). The formative loss; see letters file.
4.  [v] 1931–34 King's College, Cambridge (mathematics); elected
    Fellow of King's 1935, age 22, on the strength of his dissertation
    on the Central Limit Theorem.
5.  [v] 1935: Max Newman's Foundations course poses the
    Entscheidungsproblem; "On Computable Numbers" follows 1936.
6.  [v] 1936–38 Princeton, PhD under Alonzo Church ("Systems of Logic
    Based on Ordinals", 1938).
7.  [v] 4 September 1939: reports to Bletchley Park (GC&CS), day after
    war declared. Head of Hut 8 (naval Enigma).
8.  [v] Spring 1941: brief engagement to Joan Clarke, fellow Hut 8
    cryptanalyst; he told her of his homosexuality; engagement ended by
    him; friendship endured.
9.  [v] OBE 1946 for war services (nature of services secret).
10. [v] 1945–48 National Physical Laboratory: ACE design — a complete
    stored-program computer specification, 1945/46.
11. [v] 1948: joins Max Newman's Computing Machine Laboratory,
    Manchester; deputy director; works with the Manchester Baby /
    Ferranti Mark I.
12. [v] August 1947: 2:46:03 marathon, fifth in the AAA championship
    (Walton Athletic Club); an injury ended Olympic prospects for 1948.
13. [v] March 1951: elected Fellow of the Royal Society (for the 1936
    work).
14. [v] December 1951–January 1952: relationship with Arnold Murray;
    burglary at Turing's Wilmslow house by an associate of Murray's;
    Turing reports it to the police and, questioned, states the
    relationship plainly. Charged under Section 11, Criminal Law
    Amendment Act 1885 ("gross indecency").
15. [v] 31 March 1952, Knutsford: pleads guilty; probation conditional
    on a year of oestrogen "organo-therapy" (stilboestrol) — chemical
    castration — chosen over prison.
16. [v] Consequence: security clearance withdrawn; GCHQ consulting
    ends; foreign travel scrutinized.
17. [v] August 1952: "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" published
    (Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B); see morphogenesis file.
18. [a] From 1952: sessions with Jungian analyst Franz Greenbaum
    (Manchester); dream journals kept in notebooks; a friendship as
    much as a treatment.
19. [v] Holidays abroad 1952–53 (Norway; Club Méditerranée, Ipsos,
    Greece) — partly seeking company he could not lawfully seek at
    home; watched by authorities [a on the surveillance detail].
20. [v] Died 7–8 June 1954, Wilmslow, cyanide poisoning, age 41.
    Inquest verdict: suicide. A part-eaten apple at the bedside was
    never tested [v]. His mother maintained accident (careless
    amateur chemistry) [v that she maintained it]. Hodges reads
    suicide staged to be deniable to his mother [a, interpretive].
