# The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis (1952) and the late work

- class: MODEL-KNOWLEDGE (see _PROVENANCE-HEADER.md)
- affect_significance: MEDIUM-HIGH — proof the mechanizing cast of mind
  was general, not confined to computation; and the work that kept him
  absorbed through the persecution year.

1. [v] "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis", Phil. Trans. R. Soc. B,
   August 1952 — reaction-diffusion theory: two chemicals ("morphogens")
   diffusing at different rates spontaneously break symmetry and form
   stable spatial patterns from near-uniform starting conditions.
2. [v] Purpose: explain how a near-spherical embryo becomes a patterned
   organism — spots, stripes, whorls, phyllotaxis — WITHOUT a designer
   or a homunculus. Pattern from local rule and diffusion alone.
3. [v] Same signature move as 1936: replace an apparently mysterious
   capacity (here, biological form) with an explicit generative
   mechanism that can be written down and, later, computed.
4. [v] He ran simulations on the Ferranti Mark I — among the first uses
   of a digital computer for a biological model.
5. [v] Long undervalued; vindicated decades later (Gierer-Meinhardt
   formalization; experimental Turing patterns in chemistry and in
   animal-coat genetics). "The daisies remain law-abiding" in the
   simulation transcript points here [s - that phrasing is the model's,
   not attested].
6. [a] He worked on plant phyllotaxis (Fibonacci spirals in
   sunflowers) in his last years, partly unpublished at his death.
7. [v] Timing matters for the affect model: the morphogenesis work
   spans exactly the arrest-trial-hormone period (1951-54). Absorption
   in a beautiful problem ran concurrent with, not instead of, the
   distress. Both are true at once - a divergence signature at the
   scale of a life.
