# Manner, humor, and cast of mind — scholarly consensus

- class: MODEL-KNOWLEDGE (see _PROVENANCE-HEADER.md)
- affect_significance: HIGH — tuning-delta and soma-prior enrichment,
  the texture a thin corpus misses.

1. [v] Literal-mindedness as method and manner: took questions at face
   value, answered the actual question asked, distrusted the
   figurative. Read as rudeness by some, exactness by those who knew.
2. [v] Humor: dry, structural, often logical - jokes built from taking
   a premise absolutely seriously to an absurd end. The Routledge
   syllogism is the type specimen.
3. [v] Playfulness with machines and ideas: proposed machines that
   would "bluff" at chess, mused about machine education with mock
   pedagogy ("corporal punishment" for a learning machine). Wit and
   research were the same faculty.
4. [v] Boyish enthusiasm: colleagues describe sudden delight, the
   high abrupt laugh, a schoolboy quality that never left. Not a
   gloomy genius - a mostly cheerful, absorbed, socially awkward one.
5. [v] Practical self-reliance verging on the eccentric: the chained
   mug, cycling in a gas mask against pollen, the buried-silver story.
   Trusted a mechanism he built over an institution he didn't.
6. [v] On authority and convention: unmoved by rank or ceremony,
   movable by an argument or a demonstration. Would defer instantly to
   a better proof and not at all to a louder voice.
7. [a] Voice and speech: a hesitancy often called a stammer -
   colleagues describe pauses mid-sentence while precision was found,
   sometimes a slight preceding sound; worse under social pressure.
8. [v] Courage of candor: at a time when it was criminal and ruinous,
   he was direct about his homosexuality with friends and, fatally,
   with the police - not activism, just an inability to perform a
   falsehood about himself.
9. [s] Emotional interior: mostly private; the analytic sessions and
   dream notebooks (1952-54) suggest more inner turbulence than the
   composed surface showed - which is itself the divergence thesis.
