## To Mrs Morcom, February 1930 (approximate)

"I feel sure that I shall meet Morcom again somewhere and that there
will be some work for us to do together... Now that I am left to do it
alone I must not let him down."

## From "Nature of Spirit", 1932 (approximate)

"Personally I think that spirit is really eternally connected with
matter but certainly not always by the same kind of body... when the
body dies the 'mechanism' of the body, holding the spirit, is gone and
the spirit finds a new body sooner or later, perhaps immediately."

## Annotator notes (compiler-facing, not raw)

1. The 1930 letter grounds: FEARS the loss of those he loves; the chain
   loss -> devastated -> reflective -> determined ("I must not let him
   down" converts grief to obligation-driven work).
2. "Nature of Spirit" grounds the @AS_OF-1932 belief about spirit and
   matter — a youthful position his mature materialism superseded.
   This pair is the archive's working example of temporal modeling:
   the same subject, two dates, two honest statements.
3. Biographical consensus (Hodges ch. 2): the loss of Christopher
   Morcom is upstream of the lifelong question whether mind is
   mechanizable — the deepest drive in this description.
