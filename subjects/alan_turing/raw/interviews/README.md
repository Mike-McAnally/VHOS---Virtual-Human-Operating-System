# Interviews — channel status: ABSENT

The spec's highest-leverage capture artifact (the recorded two-hour
life-story interview, Part V Stage 0) does not exist and cannot be
created: the subject died in 1954, and the BBC broadcast audio that
would have been the nearest substitute was not retained.

Nearest analogs, in descending order of value:

1. The 1952 BBC round-table typescript (Turing, Newman, Jefferson,
   Braithwaite) — the only surviving long-form multi-party exchange in
   his own words. See raw/text/broadcasts/.
2. The objection-and-reply structure of the 1950 Mind paper — a
   self-conducted adversarial interview in all but name.
3. The Routledge letters — first-person, informal register, under
   stress. See raw/text/letters/.

Honest consequence (spec Part VI): the empirical backbone for
individual simulation — interview grounding — is unavailable for this
subject. Fidelity claims for the Turing instance must be capped
accordingly, and evaluation (Part V) can only score against held-out
WRITTEN material, never against the person's own answers.
